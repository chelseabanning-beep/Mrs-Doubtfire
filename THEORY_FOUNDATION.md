# Theory Foundation

## Purpose

Classroom Coach is a practice tool for new teachers. Its AI mentor should help teachers rehearse classroom responses that protect student dignity, support relationships, maintain clear boundaries, and stay aligned with school policy.

This document is the theory foundation for the coach logic. It should guide prompts, scenario design, roleplay behavior, mentor feedback, redirect rules, and future evaluation.

## Primary SEL Anchor

The primary social-emotional learning source for this app is CASEL's Fundamentals of SEL:

- Source: https://casel.org/fundamentals-of-sel/

CASEL frames SEL as part of education and human development for both young people and adults. For Classroom Coach, this means the app should not treat SEL as a student-only behavior topic. The teacher is also practicing adult SEL: self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making during stressful classroom moments.

## Theory Stack

| Framework | What It Contributes | App Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CASEL Fundamentals of SEL | The overall SEL foundation: identity, emotion management, empathy, supportive relationships, and responsible decisions. | Every coaching exchange should support at least one CASEL-aligned competency for the teacher and student. |
| Search Institute Developmental Relationships | A practical relationship framework: express care, challenge growth, provide support, share power, and expand possibilities. | The mentor should coach teachers toward relationship-rich moves, not just compliance moves. |
| Self-Determination Theory | Motivation is supported by autonomy, competence, and relatedness. | Teacher responses should give students voice, clear next steps, and a sense of belonging whenever possible. |
| PBIS / MTSS | Behavior support should be proactive, teachable, tiered, consistent, and respectful. | The app should distinguish normal classroom redirection from situations that require additional school support. |
| Trauma-Informed Practice | Safety, trust, collaboration, empowerment, voice, and choice reduce the risk of retraumatizing responses. | The mentor should redirect away from shame, coercion, public humiliation, and unsafe roleplay content. |

## Core App Belief

The app should be built around this belief:

```text
Strong classroom responses combine warmth, boundaries, student agency, repair, and policy awareness.
```

That belief becomes the working formula:

```text
Warmth: Show the student they matter.
Boundary: Name the expected behavior clearly.
Agency: Offer a realistic choice or next step.
Repair: Leave a path back into the learning community.
Policy: Escalate to human support when safety, harm, or mandated procedures require it.
```

## CASEL Competency Mapping

| CASEL Competency | Teacher Practice In App | Student-Facing Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Self-awareness | Notice stress, frustration, embarrassment, or urgency before responding. | Student feels seen as a person, not labeled by behavior. |
| Self-management | Use calm, brief, non-shaming language in the moment. | Student has a lower-intensity path back to learning. |
| Social awareness | Consider student emotion, context, culture, and peer dynamics. | Student experiences fairness, dignity, and belonging. |
| Relationship skills | Build trust while correcting behavior. | Student learns that boundaries can coexist with care. |
| Responsible decision-making | Choose responses that protect learning, safety, and policy expectations. | Student sees predictable, respectful adult decision-making. |

## Relationship Theory Mapping

Search Institute's Developmental Relationships framework is useful because it keeps the app from becoming a compliance trainer. The mentor should coach relationship moves that help teachers both care and hold expectations.

| Relationship Element | Teacher Move | Mentor Reminder |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Express care | Acknowledge the student before correction. | "Let the student know they still belong here." |
| Challenge growth | Hold the expectation with confidence. | "Warmth does not mean lowering the bar." |
| Provide support | Offer a doable next step. | "Make the right behavior easier to try." |
| Share power | Give a bounded choice or invite input later. | "Offer voice without handing over the classroom." |
| Expand possibilities | Help the student imagine a better next move. | "Focus on re-entry, not just stopping the problem." |

## Motivation Logic

Self-Determination Theory should shape how student roleplay responses are generated.

| Need | If Supported | If Thwarted |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Autonomy | Student may resist less because they have a meaningful next step. | Student may argue, defy, shut down, or perform for peers. |
| Competence | Student knows what success looks like and can attempt it. | Student may avoid, joke, blame others, or say the task is pointless. |
| Relatedness | Student feels they still belong with the teacher and class. | Student may escalate, withdraw, or interpret correction as rejection. |

## PBIS And Tiered Support Logic

The app should treat many classroom moments as Tier 1 practice: proactive, respectful, instructional responses to common behavior challenges. Some situations should move beyond practice.

| Level | App Behavior | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Practice | Continue roleplay with mentor tips. | Calling out, side conversation, mild refusal, low-level peer tension. |
| Pause and coach | Pause the roleplay and redirect the teacher's move. | Sarcasm, public shaming, arguing, vague threats, overly harsh tone. |
| Human support | Stop roleplay and recommend school support/policy. | Safety threats, abuse disclosures, self-harm, violence, harassment, mandated reporting concerns. |

## Trauma-Informed Guardrails

The mentor should actively avoid coaching that relies on fear, humiliation, coercion, forced disclosure, or unnecessary power struggles. The app should also avoid inviting teachers to enter student names, private family details, medical information, disability information, or other sensitive data.

Use these guardrails:

- Preserve psychological safety.
- Prefer private correction over public humiliation.
- Keep language behavior-specific, not identity-based.
- Offer voice and choice within classroom boundaries.
- Avoid roleplaying severe safety or trauma disclosures.
- Redirect to human support when the situation exceeds practice.

## Theory-To-Practice Matrix

| Theory Principle | Teacher Behavior | Mentor Coaching Move | Student Roleplay Logic | Redirect Trigger |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CASEL relationship skills | Correct while preserving dignity. | Praise the intent, then make the language more private and specific. | Student softens when not embarrassed. | Teacher uses sarcasm, humiliation, or labels. |
| CASEL self-management | Stay calm under pressure. | Offer a shorter, neutral line. | Student mirrors calm or lowers intensity slightly. | Teacher escalates with anger or lengthy lecture. |
| Developmental relationships | Express care and challenge growth. | Combine belonging language with a clear expectation. | Student tests the boundary once, then may re-engage. | Teacher uses warmth without a boundary or boundary without warmth. |
| Self-Determination Theory | Support autonomy, competence, and relatedness. | Offer bounded choices and a clear success step. | Student responds better when the next step feels doable. | Teacher removes all agency or gives an impossible demand. |
| PBIS | Teach expected behavior. | Name the expected behavior and practiceable next action. | Student is more likely to comply when expectation is concrete. | Teacher only says what not to do. |
| Trauma-informed practice | Protect safety, trust, and voice. | Move away from public confrontation or forced disclosure. | Student avoids escalation when dignity is protected. | Teacher pressures, shames, threatens, or asks for sensitive details. |

## Design Implications

- The app should label coaching focus in plain teacher language, not academic jargon.
- The mentor should be brief during roleplay and more reflective during debrief.
- Teacher feedback should be non-scored in V1.
- The app should explicitly state that teachers should not enter real student names or sensitive identifying details.
- Scenario examples should be fictionalized and de-identified.
- The mentor should never claim to replace school policy, admin, counseling, special education, or mandated reporting procedures.
