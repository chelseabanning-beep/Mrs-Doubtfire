# Demo Flow

## Purpose

This is the first 2-3 minute demo flow for Classroom Coach. It is designed to show the app promise quickly:

```text
Confidence to teach.
```

The demo should show that a teacher can enter a classroom moment, get a safe roleplay, practice a response, receive a short mentor tip, retry, and leave with a more confident line.

## Demo Defaults

| Setting | Choice |
| --- | --- |
| Audience | General educator audience |
| Grade band | 3-5 |
| Scenario | Calling out during instruction |
| Mentor tone | Adaptive, defaulting to warm instructional coach |
| Coaching intensity | Balanced |
| Student difficulty | Realistic: resists once, then softens |
| Feedback style | Student first, then short Mentor Tip |
| Policy language | General now, handbook-grounded later |
| Length | 2-3 minutes |

## User Does Not Need To Choose

The demo should not ask:

- What kind of educator are you?
- What mentor style do you want?
- How hard should the student be?

Those defaults are handled by the app.

## Demo Story

A grades 3-5 teacher is modeling a math strategy. One student keeps calling out answers while other students are raising hands. The teacher wants to redirect the student without embarrassing them or losing momentum.

## Step 1: Intake

Teacher enters:

```text
I teach fourth grade. During math instruction, one student keeps calling out answers while I am modeling. I want to redirect them without embarrassing them.
```

App response:

```text
Coaching focus: Use warmth, one clear boundary, and a path back.

We will practice a grades 3-5 calling-out moment. The goal is to help the student feel heard while keeping one voice during instruction.

Ready to start?
```

## Step 2: Roleplay Setup

Student:

```text
Oh! I know it! The answer is 24!
```

Mentor Tip:

```text
Try a short line that acknowledges the student's thinking, sets the one-voice expectation, and promises a turn soon.
```

## Step 3: Teacher First Attempt

Demo teacher intentionally gives a weak-but-common response:

```text
Stop calling out. You are interrupting everyone.
```

Student:

```text
But I knew the answer! I was just helping.
```

Mentor Tip:

```text
Your boundary is clear, but the student may hear it as rejection. Add warmth and a path back: "I want to hear your thinking. Right now I need one voice, and I will come back to you after this example."
```

## Step 4: Teacher Retry

Teacher tries the improved response:

```text
I want to hear your thinking. Right now I need one voice, and I will come back to you after this example.
```

Student:

```text
Okay... can you call on me next?
```

Mentor Tip:

```text
Strong repair. You kept the expectation and gave the student a real path back into participation. Follow through by inviting them in when it fits.
```

## Step 5: Short Debrief

App response:

```text
What worked:
You protected the student's dignity while keeping the lesson boundary clear.

Try next:
Keep the redirect brief so instruction keeps moving.

Practice line:
"I want to hear your thinking. Right now I need one voice, and I will come back to you after this example."

Confidence check:
Would you feel ready to try this line in a real classroom moment: not yet, somewhat, or yes?
```

## Step 6: Confidence Check

Teacher selects:

```text
Somewhat.
```

App response:

```text
That is a good place to be after one practice round. The next step is repetition: try the same line once more, a little shorter and calmer.
```

## Timing

| Segment | Target Time |
| --- | --- |
| Intake | 20-30 seconds |
| Setup | 20 seconds |
| First attempt | 30 seconds |
| Mentor coaching | 30 seconds |
| Retry | 30 seconds |
| Debrief and confidence check | 30-45 seconds |

Total: 2-3 minutes.

## Demo Success Criteria

The demo succeeds if observers understand:

- The app is for teacher practice, not student surveillance.
- The teacher does not need to know SEL theory to use it.
- The mentor gives concrete language, not vague advice.
- The student response changes based on the teacher's move.
- The app balances warmth and boundaries.
- The app is careful about safety, privacy, and policy.

## Optional Presenter Notes

Use this explanation if presenting live:

```text
Classroom Coach helps teachers practice the moment before it happens. This demo focuses on a common grades 3-5 scenario: calling out during instruction. The AI plays the student and also provides a short mentor tip. The goal is not to score the teacher. The goal is to help them leave with a line they can actually use.
```

## Future Demo Variations

After the first demo works, add:

- Refusal to start work.
- Side conversations.
- Peer conflict.
- Student says "this is stupid."
- Teacher repair after a tense moment.
- Handbook-grounded policy support.
